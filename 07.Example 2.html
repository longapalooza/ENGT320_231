07.Example 2¶

Given:¶

A major objective in advancing gas turbine engine technologies is to increase the temperature limit associated with operation of the gas turbine blades. This limit determines the permissible turbine gas inlet temperature, which, in turn, strongly influences overall system performance. In addition to fabricating turbine blades from special, high-temperature, high-strength superalloys, it is common to use internal cooling by machining flow channels within the blades and routing air through the channels. We wish to assess the effect of such a scheme by approximating the blade as a rectangular solid in which rectangular channels are machined. The blade, which has a thermal conductivity of $ k=25 \frac{W}{mK} $, is $ 6 mm $ thick, and each channel has a $ 2 mm \times 6 mm $ rectangular cross section, with a $ 4 mm $ spacing between adjoining channels.

Required:¶

Under operating conditions for which $ h_o = 1000 \frac{W}{m^2 K} $, $ T_{\infty, o} = 1700 K $, $ h_i = 200 \frac{W}{m^2 K} $, and $ T_{\infty, i} = 400 K $, determine the temperature field in the turbine blade. At what location is the temperature a maximum?

To begin the analysis, the turbine blade body needs to be discretized. Recognizing the symmetry in the pattern of channels in the turbine body blade, the turbine body blade may be discretized using $ \Delta x = \Delta y = 1 mm $ elements as shown below.

In [4]:
delx = Q_(1, 'mm')

At this point, each node must have a governing equation expressed for it. Beginning with node 1, it may be seen that this is a new case that has yet to be discussed. Thus, the governing equation must be derived. Isolating node 1 and labeling the heat flows into the element is shown below.

It may then be seen that the heat flows are
    $ q_1 = 0 $
    $ q_2 = k \left( \frac{\Delta x}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \frac{T_7 - T_1}{\Delta y} $
    $ q_3 = k \left( \frac{\Delta y}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \frac{T_2 - T_1}{\Delta x} $
    $ q_4 = h_o \left( \frac{\Delta x}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \left( T_{\infty,o} - T_1 \right) $
Combining these expression together for the energy balance yields
    $ k \left( \frac{\Delta x}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \frac{T_7 - T_1}{\Delta y} + k \left( \frac{\Delta y}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \frac{T_2 - T_1}{\Delta x} + h_o \left( \frac{\Delta x}{2} \cdot 1 \right) \left( T_{\infty,o} - T_1 \right) = 0$
Simplifying shows
    Node 1: $ T_2 + T_7 - \left(2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_1 = -\frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
It may be seen that nodes 6, 18, and 21 are comprable geometry so a similar approach and expression may be used. These are shown below.
    Node 6: $ T_5 + T_{12} - \left(2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_6 = -\frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    Node 18: $ T_{17} + T_{12} - \left(2 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) T_{18} = -\frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $
    Node 21: $ T_{15} + T_{20} - \left(2 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) T_{21} = -\frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $
Continuing through the nodes, shows that nodes 2 through 5, 16, and 17 correspond to case 3. Thus, they are
    Node 2: $ T_1 + T_3 + 2T_8 - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_2 = - 2 \frac{ h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    Node 3: $ T_2 + T_4 + 2T_9 - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_3 = - 2 \frac{ h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    Node 4: $ T_3 + T_5 + 2T_{10} - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_4 = - 2 \frac{ h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    Node 5: $ T_4 + T_6 + 2T_{11} - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) T_5 = - 2 \frac{ h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    Node 16: $ T_{15} + T_{17} + 2T_{10} - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) T_{16} = - 2 \frac{ h_i \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $
    Node 17: $ T_{16} + T_{18} + 2T_{11} - 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) T_{17} = - 2 \frac{ h_i \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $
Continuing through the nodes, shows that nodes 7, 12, 13, and 20 are a case 3 with adiabatic conditions. Thus, they are
    Node 7: $ T_{1} + 2T_{8} + T_{13} - 4 T_{7} = 0 $
    Node 12: $ T_{6} + 2T_{11} + T_{18} - 4 T_{12} = 0 $
    Node 13: $ T_{7} + 2T_{14} + T_{19} - 4 T_{13} = 0 $
    Node 20: $ T_{19} + 2T_{14} + T_{21} - 4 T_{20} = 0 $
Continuing through the nodes, shows that nodes 8 through 11 and 14 are case 1. Thus, they are
    Node 8: $ T_2 + T_7 + T_9 + T_{14} - 4T_8 = 0 $
    Node 9: $ T_3 + T_8 + T_{10} + T_{15} - 4T_9 = 0 $
    Node 10: $ T_4 + T_9 + T_{11} + T_{16} - 4T_{10} = 0 $
    Node 11: $ T_5 + T_{10} + T_{12} + T_{17} - 4T_{11} = 0 $
    Node 14: $ T_8 + T_{13} + T_{15} + T_{20} - 4T_{14} = 0 $
Continuing through the nodes, shows that node 15 is case 2. Thus, it is
    Node 15: $ 2T_9 + 2T_{14} + T_{16} + T_{21} - 2 \left( 3 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) T_{15} = - 2 \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $
Lastly, it may be seen that node 19 is case 4 with adiabatic conditions. Thus, it is
    Node 19: $ T_{13} + T_{20} - 2 T_{19} = 0 $

Now that the governing equations have all been stated, they must be gathered into the matrix form. It is sometimes helpful to recognize recurring terms and define them as simplier variables. The terms that commonly occur are shown below.
    $ a = 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} $
    $ b = 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} \right) $
    $ c = 2 \left( 3 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) $
    $ d = 2 \left( 2 + \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} \right) $
    $ e = 2 + \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} $
    $ f = \frac{h_o \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,o} $
    $ g = \frac{h_i \Delta x}{k} T_{\infty,i} $

f = (h_o*delx/k)*T_inf_o
g = (h_i*delx/k)*T_inf_i
