13.Example 2¶

Given:¶

The spectral distribution of surface irradiation is shown below.

Required:¶

What is the total irradiation?

Solution:¶

Start first by importing any needed libraries.

In [1]:
import pint # https://pint.readthedocs.io

The unit registry may then be defined. This is shown below.

In [2]:
ureg=pint.UnitRegistry()
Q_=ureg.Quantity

The key points of the plot are defined below.

In [3]:
lambda_0=Q_(0, 'um')
G_0=Q_(0, 'W/(m**2*um)')
lambda_1=Q_(5, 'um')
G_1=Q_(1000, 'W/(m**2*um)')
lambda_2=Q_(20, 'um')
G_2=G_1
lambda_3=Q_(25, 'um')
G_3=G_0

The total irradiation is given by $$G=\int_0^\infty G_\lambda d\lambda$$ This integral may be broken down piecewise as shown below. $$G=\int_0^{5\mu m} G_\lambda d\lambda+\int_{5\mu m}^{20\mu m} G_\lambda d\lambda+\int_{20\mu m}^{25\mu m} G_\lambda d\lambda+\int_{25\mu m}^\infty G_\lambda d\lambda$$ This may be simplified by performing each of the integral. Recall that an integral represents the area under a curve. Since the curves in the various parts are primitive shapes, their area is readily obtained. This is shown below. $$G=\frac{1}{2}\left(G_1-G_0\right)\left(\lambda_1-\lambda_0\right)+G_1\left(\lambda_2-\lambda_1\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(G_2-G_3\right)\left(\lambda_3-\lambda_2\right)$$ Notice also that the last integral goes to zero because there is no area under the curve from $25\mu m$ to infinity. Calculating all of this is shown below.

In [4]:
G=0.5*(G_1-G_0)*(lambda_1-lambda_0)+G_1*(lambda_2-lambda_1)+0.5*(G_2-G_3)*(lambda_3-lambda_2)
round(G.to('kW/m**2'), 2)    # answer #
Out[4]:
20.0 kilowatt/meter2
