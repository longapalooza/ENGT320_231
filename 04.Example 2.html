04.Example 2¶

Given:¶

A bismuth-antimony thermocouple reads a voltage difference of $0.1 mV$. The Seebeck coefficients of bismuth and antimony are $-72 \mu V/K$ and $47 \mu V/K$, respectively.

Required:¶

If the temperature of the reference junction is known to be $22 ^\circ C$, what is the temperature of the measuring junction?

Solution:¶

Start first by importing any needed libraries.

In [1]:
import pint # https://pint.readthedocs.io

The unit registry may then be defined. This is shown below.

In [2]:
ureg = pint.UnitRegistry()
Q_ = ureg.Quantity

The given problem variables may now be defined with units.

In [3]:
delV = Q_(0.1, 'mV')
S_Bi = Q_(-72, 'uV/K')
S_Sb = Q_(47, 'uV/K')
T_ref = Q_(22, 'degC')

The difference of the Seebeck coefficients is defined as
    $S_{Sb}-S_{Bi}=\frac{-\Delta V}{\Delta T}=\frac{-\Delta V}{T_{ref}-T_m}$
Solving for the measured temperature $T_m$ shows
    $T_m=T_{ref}+\frac{\Delta V}{S_{Sb}-S_{Bi}}$

In [4]:
T_m = T_ref.to('K') + delV/(S_Sb - S_Bi)
round(T_m.to('degC'), 1)    # answer
Out[4]:
22.8 degree_Celsius
