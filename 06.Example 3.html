06.Example 3¶

Given:¶

To generate a power of $P_{gen} = 9 W$, the temperature of a fuel cell must be maintained at $T_c = 56.4 ^\circ C$, which requires removal of $11.25 W$ from the fuel cell and a cooling air velocity of $V = 9.4 \frac{m}{s}$ for $T_\infty=25^\circ C$. To provide these convective conditions, the fuel cell is centered in a $50 mm \times 26 mm$ rectangular duct, with $10 mm$ gaps between the exterior of the $50 mm \times 50 mm \times 6 mm$ fuel cell and the top and bottom of the well insulated duct wall. A small fan, powered by the fuel cell, is used to circulate the cooling air. Inspection of a particular fan vendor’s data sheets suggests that the ratio of the fan power consumption to the fan’s volumetric flow rate is $\frac{P_f}{V_f} = C = 1000 \frac{W}{\frac{m^3}{s}}$ for the range of $10^{-4} \leq V_f \leq 10^{-2} \frac{m^3}{s}$.

Consider the effect of attaching an aluminum ($k = 200 \frac{W}{mK}$) finned heat sink, of identical top and bottom sections, onto the fuel cell body. The base of the heat sink is of thickness $t_b = 2 mm$. Each of the $N$ rectangular fins is of length $L_f = 8 mm$ and thickness $t_f = 1 mm$, and spans the entire length of the fuel cell, $L_c = 50 mm$. With the heat sink in place, radiation losses are negligible and the convective heat transfer coefficient may be related to the size and geometry of a typical air channel by an expression of the form $h = 1.78 k_{air} \left(\frac{L_f + a}{L_fa}\right)$, where $a$ is the distance between fins.

Required:¶

  1. Determine the net electric power produced by the fuel cell fan system, $P_{net} = P_{gen} - P_f$.
  2. Draw an equivalent thermal circuit for part 2 and determine the total number fins needed to reduce the fan power consumption to half of the value found in part 1.

The thermal circuit of the fin arrangement is shown below.

Before the circuit is analyzed, a few things need to discussed. There is no closed form solution to find the exact number of fins. Even if there were a closed form solution, a fin count $N$ that is something other than an integer is not reasonable. Since $N$ must be an integer (as well as an even integer because there are two sides; it would be strange to have different number of fins on each side), a guess and check method is a reasonable approach. Before the guess and check method begins, any parameter that is not dependent on the number of fins $N$ may first be determined. The first parameter that is not dependent on the number of fins $N$ is the thermal resistance of the base and is be found by
    $R_{t,base}=\frac{t_b}{k_{al}\left(2 L_c W_c\right)}$

In [9]:
