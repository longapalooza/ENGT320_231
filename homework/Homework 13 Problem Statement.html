Do the following in engineering format.

  1. The spectral distribution of the radiation emitted by a diffuse surface may be approximated as follows.
    1. What is the total emissive power?
      Answers: $ 2000 \frac{W}{m^2} $
    2. What is the total intensity of the radiation emitted in the normal direction and at an angle of 30 from the normal?
      Answers: $ 637 \frac{W}{m^2 sr} $
    3. Determine the fraction of the emissive power leaving the surface in the directions $ \frac{\pi}{4} \geq \theta \geq \frac{\pi}{2} $.
      Answers: $ 0.5 $
  2. Consider an opaque horizontal plate that is well insulated on its back side. The irradiation on the plate is $2500 \frac{W}{m^2}$, of which $500 \frac{W}{m^2}$ is reflected. The plate is at $227 ^\circ C$ and has an emissive power of $1200\frac{W}{m^2}$. Air at $127 ^\circ C$ flows over the plate with a heat transfer convection coefficient of $15 \frac{W}{m^2}K$. Determine the emissivity, absorptivity, and radiosity of the plate. What is the net heat transfer rate per unit area?
    Answers: $ 0.34 $, $ 0.8 $, $ 1700 \frac{W}{m^2} $, and $ -700 \frac{W}{m^2} $
  3. A horizontal semitransparent plate is uniformly irradiated from above and below, while air at $T_\infty=310 K$ flows over the top and bottom surfaces, providing a uniform convection heat transfer coefficient of $h = 50 \frac{W}{m^2K}$. The absorptivity of the plate to the irradiation is $0.40$. Under steady-state conditions measurements made with a radiation detector above the top surface indicate a radiosity (which includes transmission, as well as reflection and emission) of $J = 5500 \frac{W}{m^2}$, while the plate is at a uniform temperature of $T = 360 K$. Determine the irradiation G and the emissivity of the plate. Is the plate gray $\left( \varepsilon = \alpha \right)$ for the prescribed conditions?
    Answers: $ 8000 \frac{W}{m^2} $, and $ 0.74 $
  4. The energy flux associated with solar radiation incident on the outer surface of the earths atmosphere has been accurately measured and is known to be $ 1368 \frac{W}{m^2} $. The diameters of the sun and earth are $ 1.39 \times 10^9 $ and $ 1.27 \times 10^7 m $, respectively, and the distance between the sun and the earth is $ 1.5 \times 10^{11} m $.
    1. What is the emissive power of the sun?
      Answer: $ 6.37 \times 10^7 \frac{W}{m^2} $
    2. Approximateing the sun's surface as black, what is its temperature?
      Answer: $ 5790 K $
    3. At what wavelength is the spectral emissive power of the sun a maximum?
      Answer: $ 0.5 \mu m $
    4. Assuming the earth's surface to be black and the sun to be the only source of energy for the earth, estimate the earth's surface temperature.
      Answer: $ 279 K $
