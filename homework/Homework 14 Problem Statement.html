Do the following in engineering format.

  1. A cylinder of $ 30 mm $ diameter and $ 150 mm $ length is heated in a large furnace having walls at $ 1000 K $, while air at $ 400 K $ is circulating at $ 3 \frac{m}{s} $. Estimate the steady-state cylinder temperature under the following specified conditions.

    (a) The cylinder is in cross flow, and its surface is diffuse and gray with an emissivity of $ 0.5 $.
    Answers: $ 839 K $

    (b) The cylinder is in cross flow, but its surface is spectrally selective with $ \alpha_\lambda = 0.1 $ for $ \lambda \le 3 \mu m $ and $ \alpha_\lambda = 0.5 $ for $ \lambda \gt 3 \mu m $.
    Answer: $ 770 K $

  2. The spectral, directional emissivity of a diffuse material at $ 2500 K $ has the following distribution:
    Determine the total, hemispherical emissivity at $ 2500 K $. Determine the emissive power over the spectral range $ 0.8 $ to $ 2.5 \mu m $ and for the directions $ 0 \le \theta \le 15 ^\circ $.
    Answers: $ 0.540 $ and $ 49.41 \frac{kW}{m^2} $
  3. A diffuse surface is characterized by the spectral hemispherical emissivity distribution shown.
    Consider surface temperatures over the range of $ 300 \le T_s \le 1000 K $, at what temperature will the emissive power be minimized? Show why.
    Answer: $ 300 K $
