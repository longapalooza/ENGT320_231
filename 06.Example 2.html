06.Example 2¶

Given:¶

The engine cylinder of a motorcycle is constructed of 2024-T6 aluminum alloy is of height $H=0.15 m$ and outside diameter $D=50 mm$. Under typical operating conditions the outer surface of the cylinder is at a temperature of $500 K$ and is exposed to ambient air at $300 K$, with a convection coefficient of $50 \frac{W}{m^2K}$. Annular fins are integrally cast with the cylinder to increase heat transfer to the surroundings. Consider five such fins, which are of thickness $t=6 mm$, length $L=20 mm$, and equally spaced.

Required:¶

What is the increase in heat transfer due to use of the fins?

Solution:¶

Start first by importing any needed libraries.

import math
import pint # https://pint.readthedocs.io

The unit registry may then be defined. This is shown below.

In [2]:
ureg = pint.UnitRegistry()
Q_ = ureg.Quantity

The given problem variables may now be defined with units.

In [3]:
H = Q_(0.15, 'm')
D = Q_(50, 'mm')
T_b = Q_(500, 'K')
T_inf = Q_(300, 'K')
h = Q_(50, 'W/(m**2*K)')
N = 5
t = Q_(6, 'mm')
L = Q_(20, 'mm')

A sketch of the cylinder is shown below.

The diagram shows two radius variables that are particularly useful in the solution. These are defined below.

In [4]:
r_1 = D/2

In [5]:
r_2 = r_1 + L

Using the average temperature of $\bar{T}=\frac{T_b+T_\infty}{2}=400K$ for the material properties, the thermal conductivity of 2024-T6 aluminum alloy may be found in table A.1. This is defined below.

In [6]:
k = Q_(186, 'W/(m*K)')

The total heat rate of a fin array is given by
    $q_t=h A_t \left[ 1- \frac{N A_f}{A_t} \left( 1 - \eta_f \right)\right]\theta_b$
The fin area is given by
    $A_f=2\pi \left( r_2^2 - r_1^2 \right)+2\pi r_2 t$

In [7]:
A_f = 2*math.pi*(r_2**2 - r_1**2) + 2*math.pi*r_2*t
round(A_f.to('m**2'), 5)
Out[7]:
0.01049 meter2

The total area is then
    $A_t=N A_f + 2\pi r_1 \left(H-Nt \right)$

In [8]:
A_t = N*A_f + 2*math.pi*r_1*(H - N*t)
round(A_t.to('m**2'), 4)
Out[8]:
0.0713 meter2

The temperature difference at the base is found by
    $\theta_b=T_b-T_\infty$

In [9]:
theta_b = T_b - T_inf
theta_b
Out[9]:
200 kelvin

To determine the fin efficiency, the summary plot for annular fins may be used. This plot requires three parameters be calculated. The first is the $L_c$ term. This is shown below.
    $L_c=L+\frac{t}{2}$

In [10]:
L_c = L + t/2
L_c
Out[10]:
23.0 millimeter

The next parameter that is required is the $A_p$ term. This is shown below.
    $A_p=L_ct$

In [11]:
A_p = L_c*t
A_p
Out[11]:
138.0 millimeter2

Using these two parameters, the $x$-axis term may be determined. This is shown below.
    $L_c^{\frac{3}{2}}\left(\frac{h}{k A_p}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}$

In [12]:
xAxisValue = L_c**(3/2)*(h/(k*A_p))**(1/2)
round(xAxisValue.to(''), 2)
Out[12]:
0.15 dimensionless

The last required parameter is the radius ratio. This is shown below.
    $\frac{r_{2c}}{r_1}$
where $r_{2c}$ is defined as
    $r_{2c}=r_2+\frac{t}{2}$
These values are found below.

In [13]:
r_2c = r_2 + t/2
r_2c
Out[13]:
48.0 millimeter
In [14]:
radiusRatio = r_2c/r_1
radiusRatio
Out[14]:
1.92 dimensionless

The plot shown below may now be used.

Reading the chart, it shows the fin efficiency is approximately $95\%$. This is defined below.

In [15]:
eta_f = 0.95

The total heat rate is then found by
    $q_t=h A_t \left[ 1- \frac{N A_f}{A_t} \left( 1 - \eta_f \right)\right]\theta_b$

In [16]:
q_t = h*A_t*(1 - ((N*A_f)/A_t)*(1 - eta_f))*theta_b
round(q_t.to('W'), 1)
Out[16]:
686.9 watt

The total heat rate without the fins is found by
    $q_{wo}=h \left(2\pi r_1 H \right)\theta_b$

In [17]:
q_wo = h*2*math.pi*r_1*H*theta_b
round(q_wo.to('W'), 1)
Out[17]:
235.6 watt

The change in heat rate is then
    $\Delta q=q_t-q_{wo}$

In [18]:
delq = q_t - q_wo
round(delq.to('W'), 1)    # answer
Out[18]:
451.3 watt
